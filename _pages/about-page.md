I am Marialexandra Horna — an economist working at the intersection of **climate finance, development, and emerging-markets policy**, currently based between New York and Lima.

## How I got here

I trained as an economist at **Universidad del Pacífico** (Peru), with a major in Development Economics and Finance, graduating in the top third of my class. My early curiosity sat at the boundary between markets and public goods: why some countries manage to channel private capital into the things they most need, and why so many do not.

That question pulled me toward **green and climate finance**. Over the past four years I have worked across four institutions that approach it from different angles — a regional development bank, a sovereign cooperation agency, a green-impact fund, and a multilateral innovation lab — and each shifted how I think about the problem.

## What I do

I spend most of my time **structuring investments and the frameworks that enable them**.

At **IDB Lab** I source and assess equity, debt, and blended-finance opportunities for climate and energy investments across seven Latin American countries — running due diligence, building financial models, and writing investment memoranda for committee. I also help manage a GEF-funded portfolio and contribute to the broader climate-investment strategy of the Lab.

Earlier, at **GPD Partners**, I managed a USD 14M portfolio in sustainable agriculture, water, and climate-resilient infrastructure, structuring 12 debt and equity transactions for SMEs and cooperatives, and leading ESG due diligence under IFC Performance Standards and IRIS+.

At **GIZ**, I co-designed **Peru's Green Taxonomy** with the Ministry of Economy and 15 commercial banks — the first comprehensive framework for green investment classification in the Andean region — and developed Peru's National Green Finance Roadmap, structuring a pipeline expected to mobilize USD 100M+ in sustainable infrastructure and adaptation finance.

Before that, at **CAF**, I supported resource mobilization efforts that secured USD 10M+ in non-reimbursable climate and biodiversity funds from the Green Climate Fund, GEF, KfW, AFD, and AECID.

A more granular view of these roles lives on the [Experience](/experience/) page, and selected initiatives are written up as case studies on [Projects](/projects/).

## What I think about

A few questions sit underneath most of my work:

- **How do you de-risk capital into emerging markets** without distorting the underlying market? Blended finance is the dominant answer, but the mechanics matter more than the label.
- **What is the right unit of climate finance policy** — sectors, instruments, geographies, or institutions? Peru's taxonomy work taught me that the answer depends on who has to use it.
- **How do you build climate-finance institutions in Latin America** that outlast political cycles and individual champions?

I write about these questions, mostly informally, on [Writing](/writing/).

## Research and credentials

My research on the causal impact of voluntary sustainability certifications on Peruvian agro-exports was published in the *Journal of Business* (Universidad del Pacífico, 2025), using propensity-score matching and difference-in-differences methods. I have presented this work in Buenos Aires and Lima, and I am a member of the **Visionarios Academic Circle**, a Latin American consortium of applied economic research.

I hold the **CFA Institute Climate Risk, Valuation, and Investing certificate**, the **Frankfurt School of Finance & Management Green Finance certificate**, and **Bloomberg Market Concepts & ESG**.

In 2025 I was selected as one of LAVCA's **Emerging Women Investors in Latin America** (306 finalists across the region), and in 2024 as a **Youth Ambassador for Climate Action** under Peru's COP operation.
