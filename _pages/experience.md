## IDB Lab — Climate Investment Associate
*Oct 2025 – Present · Peru / Latin America*

**Context.** IDB Lab is the innovation laboratory of the Inter-American Development Bank Group, the largest source of development finance for Latin America and the Caribbean. The Lab uses equity, debt, blended finance, and technical assistance to back climate, inclusion, and digital-economy ventures across the region.

**What I do.** I source and assess investment opportunities for climate and energy across seven Latin American countries — running due diligence, building financial models and market analyses, and writing investment memoranda for internal committees. I structure equity, debt, and blended-finance transactions in coordination with governments, accelerators, and private investors, designing mechanisms that de-risk capital flowing into climate-resilient agriculture, the circular economy, and clean technology. I help manage and monitor a portfolio co-financed with the Global Environment Facility (GEF), conducting risk assessments and supporting technical assistance to strengthen governance and financial performance. I also contribute to the development and execution of the Lab's broader climate-investment strategy, supporting pipeline development and alignment across regional operations.

**What this role gives me.** A direct view of how a multilateral innovation lab moves capital — what gets approved, what gets de-risked, and how policy and finance constraints interact at the deal level.

## GPD Partners — Green Investment Analyst
*Apr 2025 – Sep 2025 · Peru*

**Context.** GPD Partners is a Peruvian investment firm focused on sustainable agriculture, water, and climate-resilient infrastructure. It operates at the intersection of impact finance and SME / cooperative finance in Andean markets.

**What I did.** I managed a USD 14M portfolio across 12 active investments, balancing financial performance targets with environmental and impact objectives. I originated and structured 12 debt and equity transactions for SMEs and agricultural cooperatives, producing the financial models and risk assessments that informed USD 6M in investment decisions. I led ESG due diligence for 8 potential investees, evaluating biodiversity impacts, climate-adaptation measures, and supply-chain vulnerabilities in high-risk geographies across Peru and Ecuador. I also developed KPI and impact-reporting frameworks aligned with IFC Performance Standards, IRIS+, and the SDGs, and provided technical assistance to portfolio companies on financial planning and climate-smart agricultural practices.

**What this role gave me.** A close-up view of impact investing in agricultural value chains — the realism of structuring deals for cooperatives, and the discipline of translating ESG frameworks into operational decisions.

## GIZ — Junior Technical Advisor
*Jul 2023 – Mar 2025 · Peru*

**Context.** GIZ is the German federal agency for international cooperation, working with the Peruvian Ministry of Economy and financial regulators on green-finance policy. My role sat in the green-finance program, which supports Peru's transition to a sustainable financial system.

**What I did.** I co-designed **Peru's Green Taxonomy** in coordination with the Ministry of Economy, financial regulators, and 15 commercial banks — the first comprehensive framework for green-investment classification in the Andean region. I developed **Peru's National Green Finance Roadmap**, structuring a pipeline of instruments expected to mobilize USD 100M+ in sustainable infrastructure and climate-adaptation finance. I facilitated 12 multi-stakeholder workshops that integrated biodiversity indicators, climate-vulnerability data, and socio-economic considerations to build consensus on technical thresholds and implementation pathways.

**What this role gave me.** A working understanding of how green-finance frameworks actually get built — the technical thresholds, the political economy of bank engagement, and the long arc of policy implementation in emerging markets.

## CAF — Institutional Funding Intern
*Oct 2022 – Jan 2023 · Peru*

**Context.** CAF — Development Bank of Latin America and the Caribbean — is a regional multilateral that mobilizes climate and biodiversity finance into Andean and Latin American countries. My internship sat in the institutional funding team, focused on resource mobilization for the climate portfolio.

**What I did.** I supported resource-mobilization efforts that secured USD 10M+ in non-reimbursable climate and biodiversity funds from the Green Climate Fund, the Global Environment Facility, KfW, AFD, and AECID. I mapped 30+ co-financing opportunities across Latin America, analyzing eligibility requirements, grant conditions, and strategic alignment with CAF's climate portfolio to optimize blended-finance operations. I drafted 5 project proposals with theory-of-change frameworks, results matrices, and monitoring indicators for sustainability initiatives in the Andean region, coordinating with 10+ international organizations on financing terms and reporting standards.

**What this role gave me.** A first hands-on exposure to multilateral resource mobilization — the grammar of donor instruments, theory-of-change frameworks, and how concessional and blended finance get assembled in practice.
