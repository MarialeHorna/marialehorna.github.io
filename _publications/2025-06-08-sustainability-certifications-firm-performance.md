This paper examines whether voluntary sustainability certifications translate into measurable financial outcomes for agribusiness firms operating in emerging markets, and how these effects vary by firm size, sector, and access to international buyers. Drawing on firm-level data from Latin America, it tests the link between certification adoption, revenue growth, and access to green and sustainability-linked finance.

The findings have direct implications for the design of sustainable-finance instruments, voluntary disclosure frameworks, and donor-backed certification programs aimed at mobilizing private capital toward climate and sustainability outcomes.
