Coauthored with Bertha Lucía Mendoza Natividad (Universidad del Pacífico).

This paper analyzes the contribution of voluntary private certifications — focusing on GlobalGAP — to the performance of non-traditional agricultural exports in Peru. The central hypothesis is that holding voluntary private certifications is positively correlated with higher export value. The empirical strategy combines Propensity Score Matching (PSM) with a Difference-in-Differences (DID) estimator to compare certified and non-certified firms.

Results show that GlobalGAP certification contributes positively to export value at the firm level, but the effect does not extend to micro-enterprises — suggesting that scale and absorptive capacity mediate the returns to voluntary sustainability standards. The findings provide new empirical evidence for the Peruvian case and inform the design of public policy aimed at promoting certifications, strengthening agricultural competitiveness, and supporting the position of Peruvian agroexports in international markets.

**Keywords:** voluntary private certifications, export performance, GlobalGAP, agricultural exports, Peru.
