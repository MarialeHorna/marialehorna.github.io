**Role:** Junior Technical Advisor, GIZ — Co-design lead
**Partners:** Peru's Ministry of Economy & Finance · Financial regulators (SBS, SMV) · 15 commercial banks
**Period:** 2023–2025
**Status:** Published

## Context

Peru lacked a unified framework to classify which economic activities qualified as "green," which limited the ability of banks and capital markets to channel investment toward climate and biodiversity objectives. Without that common language, both private capital and public incentives moved on inconsistent definitions, raising the cost and risk of green-finance operations.

## What I did

I co-designed Peru's Green Taxonomy in close coordination with the Ministry of Economy and Finance, the country's financial regulators, and 15 commercial banks. The work involved defining sectoral scope, building technical thresholds for eligibility (with biodiversity indicators, climate-vulnerability data, and socio-economic considerations), facilitating 12 multi-stakeholder workshops to align banks and regulators on the thresholds, and translating the framework into a usable document that banks could embed into their internal credit and reporting processes.

## Outcome

The taxonomy is the **first comprehensive framework for green investment classification in the Andean region**, providing a shared technical reference for banks, regulators, and the public sector. It opens the door for subsequent instruments — green credit lines, sustainability-linked products, and disclosure standards — to be built on a coherent definition of what counts as green in the Peruvian context.
