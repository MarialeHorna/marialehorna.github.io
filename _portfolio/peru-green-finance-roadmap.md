**Role:** Junior Technical Advisor, GIZ
**Partners:** Peru's Ministry of Economy & Finance · Multilateral and bilateral financiers
**Period:** 2023–2025
**Status:** Adopted

## Context

After the Green Taxonomy laid the definitional groundwork, the next constraint was operational: which instruments, in which order, would actually mobilize capital into Peru's priority sectors for climate and adaptation finance? A roadmap was needed to sequence instruments, align stakeholders, and anchor expectations on volume and impact.

## What I did

I developed Peru's National Green Finance Roadmap, structuring a pipeline of instruments spanning sovereign green bonds, blended-finance vehicles, green credit lines, and concessional facilities. My contribution included sectoral prioritization (climate-resilient infrastructure, adaptation, biodiversity), instrument design, sequencing across institutions, and articulation of expected mobilization volumes. The roadmap was developed in coordination with the Ministry and tested with potential financiers.

## Outcome

The roadmap structures a pipeline expected to **mobilize USD 100M+ in sustainable infrastructure and climate-adaptation finance**, and provides a multi-year framework for Peru's transition toward a sustainable financial system. It is now a reference document for both domestic and multilateral stakeholders working on Peruvian green finance.
